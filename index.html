What We Do

Two systems for higher-output teams

We combine workflow automation with team enablement so AI becomes part of how work actually gets done, not just another tool sitting on the shelf.

Workflow Automation

We identify the repetitive work that creates drag, map the actual process, and build practical AI-assisted systems with human review points where judgment still matters.

  • Workflow audit and opportunity mapping
  • AI-assisted routing, drafting, summarization, and QA
  • Human-in-the-loop review checkpoints
  • Performance tracking and iteration

Team AI Enablement

Adoption is where most AI efforts fail. We help your team understand when to use AI, how to review outputs, and how to integrate new workflows without unnecessary friction.

  • Role-based training and rollout support
  • Operating guidelines and QA standards
  • Tool selection aligned to your current stack
  • Optimization based on real usage
Process

From workflow audit to first impact in 8 weeks

The goal is not to install AI everywhere. It is to target the right workflows, implement safely, and give the team a system they will actually trust and use.

01
Week 1–2

Discover

Audit workflows, interview stakeholders, identify repetitive work, risk areas, and quality bottlenecks.

02

03

04
Ongoing

Optimize

Track adoption, time savings, quality gains, and refine the system as team usage matures.

Featured case study · Insurance operations

A 30-person claims team cut reporting time, improved consistency, and reclaimed capacity.

Sagent-AI redesigned a recurring reporting and review workflow that relied on manual gathering, formatting, and handoff coordination. The new system standardized intake, drafted reports, flagged exceptions, and preserved human review on critical decisions.

-89% Report generation time
3,480 Hours freed annually
$174k Estimated annual savings
“Our team now spends time actually helping customers instead of filling out the same materials over and over. Morale is up, and the process is more consistent than it used to be.” VP of Claims Operations

What changed

Manual collection steps were consolidated, repetitive writing tasks were drafted automatically, and QA checkpoints were made explicit instead of depending on tribal knowledge.

Why it worked

The workflow matched how the team already operated. That meant less resistance, faster adoption, and better day-one usability than a tool-first rollout.

What the client got

A workflow audit, implementation roadmap, production-ready operating flow, training sessions, and a measurable outcomes dashboard for ongoing review.
